Four RLVR runs. One that learns.

A walk through what had to go right.

Bottom line: RLVR training is a tough cookie. Modern instruct models are already strong — adding on top requires three variables to line up at once: baseline in the variance band, enough non-dead groups, and a policy update budget that actually lets the policy move. Runs 1–3 each missed one. Run 4 hit all three — and the KL ceiling is near the drift zone even so.

The three constraints GRPO needs

1
Baseline in the variance sweet spot. Not so low that every completion fails (Run 1: 2.7%), not so high that every completion passes (Run 2: 82.6%).
2
Enough non-dead groups. For baseline p and group size G, the all-same share is pG + (1−p)G. Empirically per-prompt difficulty clusters outcomes, so the observed share is higher than the independence model predicts.
3
A policy update budget that lets the policy move. Learning rate, KL coefficient β, step count. Runs 1–3 all used lr=5e-6, β=0.05, 200 steps — and the policy never meaningfully moved (Run 3's KL max was 0.0011). Reward variance was necessary but not sufficient.

The takeaway

Run 4 validated the hypothesis. Same model, same G, same reward setup as Run 3 — only the policy update budget changed (lr 4×, β ÷10, steps 2×). KL max went from 0.0011 → 0.032 (29×). pass@1 went 58.53% → 60.35% (+1.82pp), the first delta outside noise across all four runs. 120 fixed, 96 regressed, net +24.

But KL max 0.032 is high enough that drift is real — 96 problems the base model used to solve are now broken. On a single-task benchmark that's still a net win; on a multi-task eval it would be the place to watch for collateral damage.

Honest caveat

Run 4's reward curve is noisy and drifts slightly down in the tail. Per-step reward is averaged over ~16 completions (2 prompts × G=8), so noise dominates the trend. The pass@1 number on 1,319 problems is the smoothed signal — that's where the +1.82pp lives. Worth flagging.

Run 3 · GSM8K · Gemma-2-2B-IT · G=8

Variance fixed · update budget still capped

Variance fixed; update budget still capped. KL max 0.0011.

Swapped to Gemma-2-2B-IT (GSM8K baseline 58.5%, smack in the middle), bumped G=8 and temperature 1.0. Observed zero-variance share fell from ~80% (OLMo) to ~52% — about 2.4× more signal per step. But with lr=5e-6, β=0.05, 200 steps, the policy barely moved: KL max 0.0011. Δ = −0.3pp, still within noise.
